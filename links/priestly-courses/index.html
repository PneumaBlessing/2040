Why This Matters

A Chronological "Time-Stamp" for Biblical Events

The unbroken continuity of the priestly course rotation from 950 BC to 70 AD provides an independent verification tool for biblical chronology. When a biblical text mentions a specific priestly course serving (such as Luke 1:5, which identifies Zechariah as belonging to "the division of Abijah"), we can precisely calculate when that event occurred. This makes the priestly course system invaluable for validating the Unified Chronology and identifying errors in conventional dating systems.
